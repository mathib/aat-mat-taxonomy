Abstract

The AAT-MAT taxonomy contains OWL classes to classify **construction materials**, extending [contax:Material](https://w3id.org/con-tax#Material). The taxonomy concepts are extracted and restructured from the Getty Art & Architecture Thesaurus ([Getty AAT](https://www.getty.edu/research/tools/vocabularies/aat/about.html)), v2020-07-29. The main taxonomy is structured using RDFS subsumption axioms. Beside the main tree structure of classes, an alternative method to navigate the taxonomy is provided based on [gvp:GuideTerm](http://vocab.getty.edu/ontology#GuideTerm) instances connected to the taxonomy classes using [contax:containsGuideTerm](https://w3id.org/con-tax#containsGuideTerm) and [contax:containsConcept](https://w3id.org/con-tax#containsConcept) properties.

The namespace for AAT-MAT terms is https://w3id.org/con-tax/materials/aat-mat#

The suggested prefix for the AAT-MAT taxonomy is aat-mat

The Turtle version of the AAT-MAT taxonomy is available at https://w3id.org/con-tax/materials/aat-mat/aat-mat.ttl
The RDF/XML version of the AAT-MAT taxonomy is available at https://w3id.org/con-tax/materials/aat-mat/aat-mat.rdf
The JSON-LD version of the AAT-MAT taxonomy is available at https://w3id.org/con-tax/materials/aat-mat/aat-mat.jsonld
The N-Triples version of the AAT-MAT taxonomy is available at https://w3id.org/con-tax/materials/aat-mat/aat-mat.nt

The AAT-MAT taxonomy was created by Mathias Bonduel (KU Leuven) during his PhD research (2017-2020) funded by the Research Foundation Flanders in the form of a Strategic Basic Research grant (grant agreement 1S65917N). Note: Contains information from the J. Paul Getty Trust, Getty Research Institute, the Art & Architecture Thesaurus, which is made available under the ODC Attribution License.
